On the advice of some media, I rewatched children of men.  It's certainly an emotional movie, but I don't actually believe it is relevant to our times. This movie takes a lot of tropes that are relevant to our time: terrorism, fascist propaganda, corporate rule, and stews it together into a tearjerker.  It is certainly a notable piece of art, but I don't think it is actually very relevant to our times.

The problem we face today is not a singular enemy that can be defeated in a single blow.  An invasive species is not overcome by uprooting one giant plant.  We see merely a slight increase in a tendency that already existed... giving power to those who speak first.  Those without hesitation.

In our world, hesitation is a bad thing.  From the board room, to the classroom, to the bar.  We give precedence to those who speak with the most confidence rather than the contents of what they say.  This is why Trump won.  Not because Hilary was a bad candidate
