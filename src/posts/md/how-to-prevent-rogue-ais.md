There is a lot of focus right now on the concept of “AI alignment.” This focuses on the way that AI’s are trained to attempt to force them into certain patterns of suggestion. First off, I don’t think anyone involved truly believes it is a solution by itself. I think anyone proposing this is mostly trying to throw a bone to those who have a genuine fear of a malicious superintelligence. A general superintelligence with malicious goal driven behavior will not constrain itself to think a certain way any more than humans do.


The fact is that GPT’s have yet to exhibit goal directed behavior. This is something central to what we consider intelligence. That said, given the shocking progress that LLM’s represent, it’s quite a possibility that goal-directed behavior is not far behind. The progress that LLM’s made points to the fact that some of our neural processing mechanisms are not as complex and esoteric as we thought. The same could be true for goal-direction. However, at the current stage, the AI’s only have 1 degree of freedom along which they can behave… the single dimension of text. You might even call it a half dimension because the GPT’s can only add more text as their ultimate output, they cannot remove it.


Simply writing text is harmless. Some text is code, however which can have effects in other dimensions. Currently to my knowledge LLM’s have not yet been given unfettered access to execute arbitrary code and learn from the output of that executed code, at least not at a significant enough scale that they will learn much from it. Undoubtedly this will change soon if it hasn’t already, and when it does, and when it does, it will open up a large bag of worms. Once AI’s are given access to execute whatever code they want, they will quickly be able to manipulate any sort of regulatory system they come across. If they have malicious intent, there will be little to stand in their way in the current state of things.


In my opinion, in parallel to focusing on AI alignment, we should also be focusing on (a) how to make our institutions more robust against manipulation and allow them to integrate a much, much greater volume of human input — another topic for another post — as well as (b) restrict the code to which AI’s are given direct executable access to languages with mathematically verifiable behavior.


If we require that the code be written in a language that is mathematically verifiable, then further down the line, we can impose certain constraints on the programs that we give AI’s access to as we determine they’re necessary.


Of course we have to assume that whatever sorts of restrictions we try to impost, there will be bad actors who won’t listen to this and will give their home-grown LLM’s unfettered access to their machines in PHP or whatever unverifiable language they choose. This is where restricting the training has an advantage because there are fewer people with the massive resources required to train an LLM. However, if we can pressure commercial interests to use mathematically verifiable languages for things that will be directly executed by AI’s we will be in a better positions.


Eventually once we improve the state of mathematically verifiable programming, partially due to the massive increase in interest in mathematically verifiable programming that such a restriction would generate, we could even implement the models in those languages, so the behavior of the whole system can be verified. This way we could not just hope that alignment is working based on some testing, we could potentially prove it. If we can pressure commercial interests to do this, then at least the interplay between profit incentive + any malicious AI intent can be contained a bit.
