For the ease of translating into mathematics, we should treat the model as a traditional information theory model.  In the last post, I described a light and a guy flipping a switch depending on the state of the light.  In the model I set up, there was one switch and one light.  However that's a bit asymmetrical.  We actually need 2 guys, A and B.  Let's give them different numbers of switches, and say each switch is connected to a light.  Each guy A and B has a set of lights, 1 for each switch.  Let's say guy A has 8 switches and guy B has 4.

Before we get into this heavy-duty, I'm going to write in [post III of this series](http://spacechimplifecom.local/2018/02/11/newtonian-physics-as-information-iii/) what this actually translates to physically in case it's hard to follow along without more of an explanation of how this could tie into something real. So if modeling 2 masses as a couple guys with switches and lights is too difficult to suspend your disbelief, then maybe that will convince you of the value in at exploring this line of thinking.
