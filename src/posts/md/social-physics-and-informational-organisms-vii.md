In all likelihood we really have to go deeper to get anything really meaningful.  There are a few things that we're going to need to fiddle with to really get this equation into something that readily allows the integration of human-scale information into any of the fundamental energy equations.

First, the sum we were using for entropy--Boltzmann's law.  There's possibly an easier way to hand that.  Since we are trying to deal with continuous cases, we can just take the wavefunction as a probability distribution and determine the entropy of that.  It turns out that this is just Von Neumann entropy.  So that is a tool that is readily available.

Determining the kinetic energy of a complex network takes something more than a mere description of each degree of freedom as a euclidean spatial dimension.  We can treat the temperature with the quantum mechanical definitions as well, however that will just lead to a rehashing of what we already have.

I think the metric is what really what needs to be analyzed here.
