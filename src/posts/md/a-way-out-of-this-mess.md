Doesn’t matter who you think made the mess, we can all agree we’re in one.

There’s a path out which involves the creation of some specific kinds of cooperative institutions.

And the responses I’ve seen that make any attempt to do something about it cluster into these groups:

*   hope AGI saves us all
    
*   hope for a savior in some form
    
*   scapegoating
    
*   hope people spontaneously start acting in concert with no incentive
    

None of these are real solutions. I can’t remember seeing a single proposal for a solution that addresses even basic tactics for how to get it done.

First we need to identify the core of the problem. If you keep asking why certain problems exist, the root is a lack of incentive alignment. People are not properly incentivized to act in each others’ interests. This naturally causes fragmentation, as people are being pulled in different directions.

This sounds abstract. Let’s make it practical. Oil company executives are not incentivized to protect our habitat. Politicians are not incentivized to improve the quality of life of their constituencies. Young urban people are not incentivized to work with people who don’t share their values. Rural people are not incentivized to pay taxes.

These cracks have been forming for a long time, but there was a glue holding those cracks together which was our functioning economy that effectively incentivized people to co-exist. COVID and AI were a combo that blew the cracks wide open, and the bit of economy that has been forming in the meantime is not adding binding to the cracks.
