A lone hydrogen atom can exist in a number of states.  If it is a cation, it can have a relative velocity with respect to any other particle, and relative distance.  It can have various nucleus configurations, but let's ignore those for the sake of focus.  To any given other particle, all that matters is the distance and the rate of change of distance, which cannot be greater than the speed of light.
