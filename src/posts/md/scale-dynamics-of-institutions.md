Before getting to the meat of this post, I want to emphasize a specific point.  I believe that ideas are merely tools, and that they have no intrinsic meaning.  They are essentially anticipatory technologies nested within each other.  Progress was made in theory and practice long before the scientific method was codified.  We all contribute to a collective consciousness and when resources are available for work on a given problem, people will start to throw out solutions.  Eventually one will provide practical benefit, and it will be stacked on top of the existing framework.  Yes, the silo effect exists between fields.  However, between any 2 given fields, there will be some people that dabble in both.  So when people are surprised at how multiple fields converge on similar ideas and use that as evidence for their validity, I find it a bit ridiculous.

Institutions exist on several scales.  Large scales are essentially unaffected by dynamics at smaller scales.  A society is essentially untouched by a given family's fortune.  This mass effect has been observed in many sciences, and as such, there are many models and tools which can be borrowed.  Unfortunately, it has been very difficult to find appropriate applications as it is difficult to measure human behavior.  Why is this?

When observing the behavior of electrons, we can create observable events involving millions of particles and nanoseconds.  This creates a ridiculous amount of data points and allows us to find statistical correlations with such confidence intervals that we can take them as linearized.  Then we can look at the astronomical scale.  These evens happen at such a slow pace that at any given point we are essentially seeing eigenvalues, or locally linearized behaviors.  As we refine our observation technology, we find error and refine our models accordingly.
