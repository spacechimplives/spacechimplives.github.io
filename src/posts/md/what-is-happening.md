Obviously there are all kinds of problems in the world, some inevitable and some caused by people's reactions to the inevitable.  Of course the world was never perfect, but things seem to be going downhill.  It's all a matter of perspective, but that's not much of a comfort when your perspective is that there is more and more uncertainty and turmoil on the horizon.

People have come up with all kinds of ways to cope with this, however it's hard to find any lifestyle that allows someone to lead a life of contentment while also working consistently toward a future that most agree is bright.

I don't remember where I heard this metaphor but sometimes it feels like we're on a ship with no captain that's headed straight for an iceberg.  But sometimes it feels worse than that.  Sometimes it feels like some of us are screaming "Iceberg, Iceberg!" and nobody is even turning their heads.  Sometimes it seems like we're chained down and gagged while others are being forced at gunpoint to keep throwing fuel into the engine to make us slam into the iceberg faster.

But the thing that is both most liberating and most horrifying to me at different times is realizing that all humans are in this same position.  Nobody wants the ship to crash into the iceberg.

Conspiracy theories come easy to me.   But I've slowly realized that they are just a scapegoat.  Sure, plenty of them may be true.  But even if they are, the reason they happen is because the people at the top are in the same boat we are.  If the government is running a huge mind control station in Alaska, it's because they're consistently incentivized to do everything the quick and dirty way.  And when the little things that were ignored to do it the quick and dirty way start going wrong in a big way, then there needs to be another quick and dirty fix.

Nobody's happy with that.  Everyone is stressed.  There is no one person or group of people we could just eliminate to get rid of the problem.  It would just pop right back up out of the ensuing chaos.

So what we need is to study the problem, but our tools for studying the problem are tainted by the same problem.  We use academia for investigations of this kind, but academia is plagued by the same grant deadlines, pressures from superiors, and general lack of openness to ideas that might create a loss.  Of course this is understandable.  A department that doesn't apply for enough grants, doesn't produce enough papers, that puts papers out that are easy targets for criticism might crash and burn.  There is no other way to run a department.

Similarly, a government that doesn't maintain a strong military might get overrun and a government that doesn't maximize investment in the military might get outpaced and the people may come into grave danger.  If that means using mind control, propaganda, authoritarianism, or whatever to prevent protests and maintain productivity, there are plenty of people around to tell those in charge that they're doing the right thing.   And there is logic to it, even though it sounds like a terrible society to live in.

So what do we do to stop this?  We have to create new tools that are independent of the system we currently have, yet are not rejected by them.  This requires studying institutions, how they influence people, how people influence them, and how they achieve goal-driven behavior.  Not only that but it requires experimenting with different possibilities for new systems.
