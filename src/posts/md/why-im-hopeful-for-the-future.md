—From where I sit, these concerns all have the same root. That root is that our institutions are not being steered. They accept inputs and produce outputs and continue until they run out of inputs. Sort of like a chemical reaction, or an algae bloom.

This is a hallmark feature of capitalism because those with capital steer the ship. But they are driven by the quest for more capital, so the ship is actually being steered by the chase for more capital.

This is understandable in a way. Just like people need food to survive, institutions also need energy. And capital is what we call that energy. That energy is processed in such a way that it produces things we want or need, and then uses those things to get us to do things. This explains the success of capitalism.

The problem comes in that there’s a hack. Instead of incentivizing us by providing things we like, a mega-capitalist can just as easily, or perhaps even more easily (in the short run anyway) incentivize us by depriving us of things we need.
