The exact timeline of human development is hard to pin down precisely but we can make some general statements.

For reference, our lineage seems to have split from chimpanzees ~6-10 million years ago.  We can guess based on various anatomical features the movement of the hyoid bone and the movement of our larynx and trachea that language has been around since at least 50-100 thousand years ago, possibly as far back as 1-2 million years ago, with some indication that this process may have just been beginning even 5 millions years ago.  The earliest stone tools seem to appear at ~3.5 million years ago.  Cooking about 1.5 million years ago.  Perhaps even clothing by this time

There is evidence of a wooden structure as far back as ~450,000 years ago, evidence of a permanent settlement as far back as 300,000 years ago, along with stone tools.  There is even evidence of long-distance trade of obsidian at that time.  Rock art at a few hundred thousand years ago.  Neanderthals (who appear to have possibly had some kind of vocal communication), lived from about 500 thousand years ago until ~40,000 years ago.

A Neanderthal flute has been found from ~60,000 years ago..  There is evidence of an ochre processing workshop as far back as 100,000 years ago, bone harpoons from 90,000 years ago.  Projectile weapons 80-100 thousand years ago.  By this time clothing was almost certainly common as evidenced by some studies on human body lice, which require it.

This is also when the Denisovans were around, Homo Florensis, as well as the earliest known migration to Australia around 65,000 years ago, which required boats and potentially navigation.  Agriculture was starting, humans were were doing burials, and creating hooks and needles.  The earliest known pottery was from ~40 thousand years ago.  Humans had certainly reached North America by ~13,000 years ago, although there are growing piles of evidence pushing it back to 20 or 30 thousand years ago.  There is unmistakable evidence of writing as far back as 6,000 years ago, but a possible candidate as far back as 20,000 years ago.

By this time humans had reached almost every part of the earth and agriculture was  starting to become commonplace.  The Natufian culture was showing signs of religion and permanent settlement, they baked bread as far back as 14,400 years ago and beer 13,000 years ago. This is also when the earliest baskets have been found.  Göbekli Tepe was built around 10,000 years ago.  Rice and beans and pigs were starting to be domesticated, then sheep and cattle.  Around 6000 years ago, writing starts to become commonplace.  Mohenjo daro was built around 5500 years ago, and the Pyramids and Sphinx were built around 4500.  At this point there was an explosion of empires all over the middle east which ended with the Bronze Age collapse. More recent history stops being important for the purposes of analyzing the role of language as institutional code.
