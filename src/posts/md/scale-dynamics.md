Next up, I will talk about how different kinds of institutions behave on different scales, their hierarchical dependence, and the codes that mediate them.

 

When objects interact, they do so at different scales.  A hydrogen atom which is part of a protein bonds covalently, but also potentially in a hydrogen bond--a weaker bond which can still be essential to the structure of the protein.  It can also interact with other molecules in concert with the rest of the protein, and may be essential to the function as well.

The smaller-scale dynamics contribute to the integrity of the structure, but as a structure gets larger, the effect of the smaller-scale elements is reduced on the whole.

I think this is due to inertia, and that the concept of inertia can be applied stochastically to human dynamics.  As a society grows to millions and millions of people, we can apply the concepts of work, power, potential energy, etc. to sociology.
