A transaction between two goods that have exactly equal value to either party would be a net increase of 0 in economic value, right?   So in order for a transaction be worth it, the utility of the good to the buyer has to be higher than the money they give, and visa versa for the seller (to make up for the work and time required to make the transaction at least).  

GDP is a representation of aggregate production.  It is supposed to represent the total economic productivity that has been output in a given period.  Presumably this value is created when a transaction has a net utility of greater than the time and energy involved in the transaction.   The sum of all these transactions should add up to GDP.
