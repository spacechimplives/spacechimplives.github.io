We can potentially then relate the efficiency of energy extraction of a given process to the channel capacity of the communication that would represent that process from an information theory perspective.

What we’d have is (Energy Extracted - Energy Spent) / (Maximum Available Energy) as the efficiency of the energy extraction process. This would be related to the channel capacity. So

(Energy Extracted - Energy Spent) / (Maximum Available Energy) = B \* log₂(1 + S/N)

Which yields

(Energy Extracted - Energy Spent) = B \* log₂(1 + S/N) \* (Maximum Available Energy)

Now, if we translate the bandwidth to total information, we multiply it by time, meaning we divide the max available energy by time… so we have information \* power.
