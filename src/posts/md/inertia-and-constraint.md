Let’s think about 2 identical masses connected by a rigid rod. Before being connected, the system can be described in terms of 4 degrees of freedom: (x1, y1) and (x2, y2). After being rigidly connected, the system can then be described with only 3: (x1, x2) and theta, representing an angle from the first particle. We can consider ourselves to have performed some kind of operation. That operation defines a new joint probability distribution for the position of A given the position of B and visa versa.

Ultimately the individual probability distributions with their individual states are combined into a new distribution with a set of shared states, and the individual distributions are a reflection of this new distribution which has a reduced dimensionality to it. In other words, its state can be encoded with fewer bits. The difference between the 2 original distributions and the new joint distribution establishes a symmetry which Noether’s theorem says will result in a conservation law. In this case it will result in the conservation of angular momentum. It seems the reduction of dimensionality comes hand in hand with the establishment of a symmetry. This appears to reflect some sort of truth about what constraints are. They must occur mutually between elements of the system, inclusive of the observer, and that mutuality establishes a symmetry.

Now let’s consider reducing that rod’s length to 0. Now we don’t even need theta, the two particles now have no degrees of freedom separate from each other. This means that any state occupied by the first will be occupied by the second. Compared to the unconstrained probability distribution, this new conditional distribution has much less entropy.
