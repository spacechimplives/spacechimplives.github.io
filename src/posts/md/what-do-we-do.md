At the end of the last post the question was "What do we do to stop the irresistible force of short-term thinking in our global society?" And the quick answer was "We have to create new tools that are independent of the system we currently have, yet are not rejected by them."

By my logic, if there were a quick, easy answer it would already be done.  But there's nothing to be done but try.  And part of trying is figuring out where to put our efforts.  I think we should not focus our efforts in our existing institutions.

Instead we need to create a new system that can be a repository for data, work, discussion, goods, and capital.  But we need to be very careful how this is designed. It needs to be designed to incentivize long-term strategies and goals.

In order to design this kind of repository, we have to understand how our current system came to be, what its strengths and weaknesses are and why they exist, and what a system even consists of.

Any efforts that are put forth into such a repository with no private cost would yield all kinds of benefits for private institutions.

To be clear, there are several of these forums or repositories that I am talking about that already exist and are widely used.  They each have strengths and weaknesses, but most of them were not initially designed to be the kinds of repositories that they became.

Wikipedia, 4chan, git, craigslist, linux, bitcoin as well as facebook, reddit  and several others are all examples of faces of the kind of repository that I'm talking about.  They allow individual humans to work together to behave collectively and store information.

We should look at how these repositories manage to keep existing despite not being optimized for profit, or how they manage to serve public interest while being profit-driven.
