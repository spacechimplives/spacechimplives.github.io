The clearest correlation with the decline of fertility rates is GDP. Almost every other hypothesis can be easily disproven by looking at one pair of cities.

Kinshasa and Brazzaville are right across the river from each other. They both dominate their respective country’s population, so statistics from either country will very likely indicate trends that are also affecting those cities. Any environmental effects will clearly impact both cities. They are also traditionally culturally very similar.

So by all hypotheses that involve environment and culture, they should have similar fertility rates. And yet they’re pretty different. They also have different GDP’s. The DRC’s is lower and its [fertility rate is higher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo). The Republic of Congo’s is higher and its [fertility is lower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_the_Republic_of_the_Congo).

What does that say? It certainly seems that economic activity itself that causes the decline of the fertility rate. This makes sense if you consider what economic activity actually is: it’s time and effort that people are giving to institutions. That’s time they cannot give to loved ones. It’s time they’re spending in institutional mode.

[This is institutional mode.](https://x.com/InternetH0F/status/1873625150106501433) In past [posts](https://spacechimplives.substack.com/p/societies-use-machine-language), I’ve described it more thoroughly, but just a quick explanation: institutions rely on people to execute policies and procedures. Policies and procedures are a form of code. Institutions rely on people to execute that code faithfully. Common sense is a liability to an institution. This institutional code is akin to the genetic code of a human. Think about how we treat our constitution, how religions treat their scriptures. It is meant to be carried out in algorithmic fashion.

The vast majority of economic activity in the world currently is mediated through the employer/employee relationship. One entity without capital submits to follow the command of another entity which has capital. This is inherently a submissive position. The result is increased cortisol, decrease sex drive, etc.

The scarce resources of a country with a high GDP are speculated upon. Real estate in particular. This means there is less room for someone to catch themselves if they fall. Start a business and don’t succeed? In the US you’re not just going to find a plot of land and grow potatoes and sell them on the side of the road until you’re back on your feet. You’ll be kicked off the land. You’ll be put in jail for selling without a permit etc. So the consequences to telling a boss to shove it when they go too far are just much higher.

What a family needs is exactly the opposite of institutional mode and this form of economic activity. It needs common sense at its core. It needs the freedom to take risks to follow a dream. What’s attractive is freedom. Freedom of thought (poetry), freedom of movement (cars), freedom to act (assertiveness). There’s no room for any of that in a society which pursues profit above all, because ultimately people spend the majority of their time in “institutional mode”, acting as automatons on behalf of their superiors.
