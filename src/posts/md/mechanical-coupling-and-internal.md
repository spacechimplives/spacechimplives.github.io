If we can accept that [mutual constraint is a form of internal energy](https://spacechimplives.substack.com/p/mutual-constraint-as-internal-energy), then we can also consider mechanical coupling to be an increase in internal energy.

How does this work? By coupling one object to another, they constrain their relative positions. This means that in exerting a force on one object, the force will get distributed to the other as well. Meaning to reach a certain acceleration, the force required will be proportional to the mass of both objects, not just one. Furthermore, the constraints that one object is subject to will be propagated to the other. If one object is constrained to a single dimension of motion, the other will be too.

In such a case, the force which is responsible for that constraint must then act on the collected mass as well.

What this means is that these objects have information about each other. In other words, in the probability density function representing their relative spatial positions, there is a single region with very high probability, and other regions with very low probability. This is by definition a low-entropy state.

Assuming the cubes are coupled, according to a coordinate system centered on the lower cube, the higher has a high probability spot at (0, 0, 3), and from the perspective of a similar coordinate system for the higher cube, the lower has a high probability spot at (0,0,-3). Everywhere else is low probability. The un-evenness of the probability density function means low entropy.

If we accept this characterization, then we can say that mechanical coupling increases the internal energy of the system. We can also say that the increase in internal energy is amplified by the pre-existing constraints.  
  
If cube A is subject to the constraint that it can only move in the Y direction, and this constraint is applied to cube B, this allows us to apply that constraint on cube A to our probability density function for cube B as well, meaning that there is more “negentropy”, or more “negentropic energy.” When we break the coupling, we can say that the resulting movement away from the constrained state will happen more quickly than if cube A was not subject to such that translational constraint.
