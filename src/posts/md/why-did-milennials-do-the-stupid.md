Some cracks are starting to show in the Gen Z self help sphere. Figures like Jordan Peterson and Andrew Tate have jumped the shark. Joe Rogan has exhausted his substantive contributions to the national dialogue. Now that the excesses of cancel culture and unconditional deference to “marginalized groups” have been effectively halted and are starting to be reversed, it’s become clear that most of them don’t have much to say worth saying outside of that context.

The seemingly dominant intellectual movements have proven to be bankrupt as well. First there was a wave that washed away people like Elizabeth Holmes and Sam Bank-Friedman, killing the “Effective Altruist” movement. Now Beff Jezos-style accelerationism has been shown to be yet another cash grab. Sam Altman, who was initially cheered on, has shown himself to be unabashedly Machiavellian. Mark Zuckerburg’s glow-up went a little too far and after some initial success is coming across for what it is: an astroturfed PR campaign

It’s unfortunate for Gen Z that their de-facto spokespeople have been either propped up due to 1 or 2 issues that made a lot of space for right-wing grifters, or Theil-funded silicon valley golden boys who have a lot to lose from biting the hands that feed them.

Why were these their spokespeople to begin with though? Gen Z has probably had more financial success than any generation since the baby boomers. Their formative years were during an overheating economy. This success made them un-suspicious of corporations. This is in contrast to millenials who were bussing tables with their degrees that were promised to pay themselves back. Who saw their loved ones’ savings wiped out, or friends ending up under bridges, and

Now much of that is crashing down.
