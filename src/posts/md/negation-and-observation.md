Observations are made by a system. They must create some physical change in the system to be registered. Otherwise they are not information.

And it can’t just create \*any\* physical change. It must create a physical change that correspond to a certain state change within the observing system. Getting hit by a basketball is not considered an observation, although many observations may be made leading up to and after the event. For such a change to be considered an observation, the system must be prepared in a certain way to translate a certain change from the environment into a specific corresponding change in internal state. The arrangement of such a mechanism implies anticipation, which implies previous physical interaction of that sort between the system and the environment. It also means that the observing system has a significantly higher than chance likelihood of arriving at a certain state given a particular state of the environment.

Now this prepared mechanism is a physical system. Therefore it must work through physical constraint. In order for the observer to reliably arrive at a in response to s and not otherwise, there must be mechanisms to filter out false positives, as well as amplification to prevent false negatives due to insufficient energy to propagate. The amplification amounts to a potential energy reservoir which empties on a trigger. The filtering can be seen in our receptors where they respond to a narrow range of stimuli by being attuned to a certain resonant frequency, or a certain energy level which is enough to change some internal part of the system configuration without exciting the entire system too much.
