Stepping back from the details, I want to note some general observations that need to be worked into whatever quantitative framework could come out of this:

Fractal nature of institutions:

The distinctions that are made on a lower level and a higher level will reflect each other.  Racial dynamics that exists in the nation will be reflected in the schools, prisons, and communities.  Obviously not exact clones, but similar to how the general structure of a cell is reiterated throughout the body, and the systems that exist within the body have correlate systems throughout the body.  This makes sense.  If metabolism requires water and sugar, then the cell will need some way of making sure those two things exist in sufficient quantities at the site of metabolism, so it will have some internal distribution mechanism.  If the cells specialize, they will be in different environments.  Each cell will still have its need for water and sugar at the site of metabolism, so the body will need some internal distribution mechanism to each cell.  If humans then live in different environments and co-depend on each other, they will need some mechanism of distributing water and sugar to each other.

The fact that it's sugar in particular that our cells use to reduce ADP to ATP, as opposed to sulfur vents or mineral reactions that some bacteria use, has significant consequences for our cells' organization, hence our bodies' organization, and thus our societies' organization.  The ultimate "goal" of metabolism--the point where potential energy stops being ratcheted up and starts being translated to kinetic energy is in working gates and channels--Calcium for the muscles, Na/K for the brain, Hydrogen for the mitochondria.
