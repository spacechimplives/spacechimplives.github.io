So one issue that people have brought up with the idea that institutions act independently of humans is the fact that individual human decisions are responsible for human actions.

This doesn't mean that there isn't something larger at play.

A protein still follows all the rules of chemistry.  You could say that a neurotransmitter like Acetylcholine "causes" sodium channels to open up in a muscle fiber.  However, that is not a sufficient causal description if you're talking about someone moving out of the way of a car.  First, the entire organism has to develop including the nervous system and muscles, then it has to exist in an environment with cars, then it has to have some way of perceiving the fact that there is a car and it is moving toward them.  The muscles contract because of the car on at least some level, in parallel to being a response to a neurotransmitter.

If we want to describe why someone shoots up a school, we're going to have to go that deep with it.  First, our society developed into a system with schools and guns.  Then, that particular individual reacted in a particular way to their circumstances.  They are reacting to the global state of our society as well as their immediate circumstances.  Of course, people don't want to take away individual responsibility from culprits.  But just slapping the label "mentally ill" on them and calling it a day is totally unhelpful.  It turns a large gray area into black and white.
