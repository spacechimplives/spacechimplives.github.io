If the probability distribution always tends toward uniform as time goes on, then how does anything push against that? How can you transform asymmetry in one probability distribution, into asymmetry in another?

Often in order to make use of an asymmetry it first must be exaggerated. Refining oil or ores, for example. Such refinement is generally done by taking advantage of some asymmetry between the desired material and undesired.

The “and” and “or” statistical operators can correspond to a series and parallel coupling into a greater probability density function representing the couples system , and mediate the effect on the probability distributions of those forms of coupling that allow the mutation of asymmetry from one form into another.

Conditional probability would represent the constrained distribution, and the difference between the conditional and unconditional probabilities would represent the increased energy of the system under constraint.
