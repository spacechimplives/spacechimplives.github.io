So how does an agent a?  How does it translate those ICP's into actions?  What physical form do these ICP's take?  How do these ICP's relate to physical quantities?

First, from posts I've written previously, we should see that an IO's ICP's will consist of policies.  In our modern society, the most blatant policies are ones that are written down.  However, different institutions have different forms.  In a family, there are policies, but they are not written down generally, and may not even be consciously referenced.

The ICP's are a form of information, so they should be able to be expressed as a quantity of bits. As I will attempt to prove, they are also a form of potential energy.

Through a hierarchical nesting of these ICP's, causal chains can be triggered and modulated to create arbitrary behavior given enough energy and storage medium, and an appropriate execution mechanism.

ICP's are essentially potential energy along a certain dimension which can be set via some calibration process.  It is probably easiest to imagine this in the form of electrical energy, however it can take all forms.  The potential energy that is used to drive the system is dissipated through the ICP's in order to cause the desired behavior.

This dissipation takes complex routes, like the dissipation of spring energy through a wind-up clock or wind-up toy.  However, unlike those two things, an IO can modulate the period and coupling of its oscillators according to triggers in the environment.

I say oscillators even though they don't necessarily oscillate.  In the case of a wind-up toy, it is rare that one starts to go backwards after coming to a rest at the end of its trajectory due to friction damping the mechanism.

A great example of a more slightly more complex model of this comes from youtuber Carykh who does some pretty awesome simulations, one of the latest of which is here.  He uses very simple building blocks and neural nets which are tied to sensors and muscles.  Using genetic algorithms, he shows these simple creatures can develop the ability to behave and react to their environments given enough time.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ghwXmA1s1I4

These examples that he creates are very close models of IO's however they are missing a few fundamental parts which are essential for translating the information they contain into physical quantities.

However he clearly points out and his models clearly illustrate the concept of ICP's.  The neural nets he uses are extracting information from the environment and translating that to movement in a direct way.

However IO's have a base amount of energy that get dissipated through the ICP's.  In the video, this could possibly be simulated by giving a certain amount of energy per food pellet and using that food pellet energy for muscle movement and neural net activity, as well as a certain amount to model maintaining homeostasis, then waiting for the energy to get to 0.

In the development of an IO, if that base energy goes too low for too long, the IO will disintegrate.  The bonds which hold the integral parts together will not maintain their force.  So the IO must acquire energy at a sufficient rate.

Physically, the IO will calibrate its ICP's in some way to represent an equilibrium at the source of energy.  In order to do that, it must have a mechanism to represent the space in which it can move.  The oscillators which store this potential energy in the body take the form of chemical gradients.  Ultimately muscles and neurons both run on potential energy in the form of chemical gradients.
