In [this post](http://spacechimplifecom.local/information-and-income-gap/), I described how money is a form of information, and how it can buy you a higher signal-noise ratio between what you intend to make happen and what happens.  But I think it would be good to try to suggest some possibilities for how to quantify this so it can be tested and move beyond the realm of nice analogizing to something worth investigating.

So first off, it's clear that dollars are energy.  We can freely convert them at the current rate of on average $0.13 per kwH.  So this would mean that the dollars per unit time are a form of power.  So a the amplitude of a signal would be measured in dollars per second, just as it's measure in joules per second elsewhere.  Similarly the noise would be measured that way as well, and the signal-noise ratio would simply be the ratio of those two.

So what would be considered the signal and what would be the noise?  Signal would be any voluntary transactions.  Noise would be involuntary.  I think a given expense could probably lie somewhere on a spectrum between those two.
