If you imagine lots of balls attached to each other with springs, and then you push one “input ball”, the momentum imparted will take time to propagate through the structure. During the process of imparting momentum, there will be push-back. As you continue to impart force on the input ball, the momentum will propagate and the structure as a whole will start to accelerate. However, viewed as a whole, the acceleration will be significantly slower than if you just pushed a single ball.

We can also imagine a large set of objects rotating around each other having similar dynamics. As you push the “input sphere,” it will be pulled back by the other spheres which are now behind it, and it will not be pulled forward as much.
