The length of a bunny's ears matter to potential bunny mates.  But it doesn't matter to a predator.

This asymmetry in the availability of information is the basis of how semi-permeable boundaries are formed within populations.

The concept of a "dog whistle" in politics, allows a group to communicate with each other in a public realm without everyone understanding.

When flirting, the fleeting glance that two potential partners give when someone else does or says something embarrassing subtley begins a syncing up that can end in a "language" that two people share with nobody else.  Inside jokes are an expression of mutual and exclusive understanding.  Perhaps this process is the similar to the formation of a new actual language.  As you spend more time communicating with a person (or animal, or anything that can communicate), you develop more shared tokens.  More shared tokens means higher information entropy, which means more information communicated per second.

More information communicated per second means asymmetries in information are dissipated more quickly, which tends to result in more "trust".  This means that more mutually beneficial transactions can occur.    I define "trust" as basically the expectation that a given interaction will be beneficial to a person.  Trust acts as a throttle for communication.  Less trust results in more information asymmetry.  In instances where agents are forced to interact despite a lack of trust, they will obfuscate.  Mutually beneficial transactions breed trust, breeding more communication, breeding more shared tokens, breeding potentially higher rate of work.

By "work" here, I mean that contributing to the "power" of a society.  By "power" I mean energy consumption, used as in the [Kardashev scale](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kardashev_scale), at least as a heuristic of the size of a civilization, but potentially as something with a bit more intrinsic meaning, if you consider life and society to be part of  a dissipative system which, tends towards an equilibrium of maximizing rate of entropy.

Trust can exist between humans, between institutions, between humans and institutions, or humans and animals.  The intensity of "phatic" communication indicates an eagerness to communicate more.  A mutually beneficial transaction can be considered one in which the "utility" invested by each party is less than the resulting utility to each party.

If two parties can communicate at a vastly higher rate with each other than they can with a 3rd party, this will result in something like a boundary.  All else equal, they will tend to transact with each other over a 3rd party because there will be higher levels of trust.  As they do this, they will amplify the asymmetry of information between the initial parties and the 3rd party.  This causes a difference in scale.  If the ease of communication between parties is represented by links of different lengths between nodes, you will see clustering towards a center.  As these clusters become more compact, the links between them will expand.

Imagine then that a given bit of information hops each link in the same amount of time.  This would result in a mathematical dynamic that parallels relativistic gravity.

A communication can be considered a kind of transaction.  Someone is boring when they communicate no more information while requiring your time.  If two agents in a network have a link that is too tight, they will have almost exactly mutual information, and there will be little need for them to communicate.  This will add an asymptotic character as two nodes approach each other.
