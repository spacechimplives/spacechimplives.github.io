This is a proposition to create the Open Party--a direct democracy party where people register and vote on an online platform and representatives agree to vote based on the outcomes of those votes.

This pandemic and the capitol riots Jan 6, 2021 have highlighted to the general public the frailty of our current social systems.  This frailty was already showing in some ways.  Despite our accelerating pace toward environmental collapse, no major steps are being taken.  Despite the increasing productivity of workers and increasing automation, the basics are less and less affordable to your average person.  And despite the protests of many different, even some powerful groups who see this, progress seems deadlocked.

So what can we do about the waning effectiveness of these systems?  To me it seems clear that everyone is locked into a set of intertwined incentives that ultimately dissipates the energy of any movement for change.  This isn't necessarily a bad thing for a system that is on track, and it is almost certainly an evolved feature of our society, but if they system is not on track, then that self-regulation can be a problem.

Why exactly are our systems failing?  In my opinion, reason number 1 has roots in information theory.  My guess is average person pretty much only votes for president once every 4 years.  That would to about .38 bits per second total getting returned to the US government about our choices.  Obviously the government gathers more data about us, there are also 3rd parties that poll us that then influence elections, which results in more bits being taken into account in the elections than just our votes.  But that actually highlights the problem.  The government, and hence the policy is not informed enough by what we actually want.

This system was designed before the telegraph and it was state-of-the-art when it came out, but these days we can do better.  First off, the reason we had representatives back then was because someone had to physically go cast a vote.  Some people argue that it's because representatives prevent a tyranny of the masses, but IMO it doesn't make sense how the masses would choose a system that limits the power of the masses.  The masses eventually win out.  Information theory explains why that system won out in the 19th century.  There are just too many decisions that needed to get made based on information from the constituency, so it made the most sense to just send someone.  Nowadays, people can vote from their computers.  That's no longer an issue.

So what I propose as a solution to this problem is: a party where people vote on each issue.  The representative is responsible for voting the way the people on the platform tell them to vote.  In order to make this happen we simply need: a website where party members can register to vote, and representatives who are willing to help implement such a system.  Perhaps at the beginning another job of theirs would be to enter all the relevant votes into the system.  People on the website may be forced to view some information from the different sides of the issues before voting, and then the representative is informed of the result, and they are required to vote that way & close the vote.

This is a simple system.  At the beginning for local elections & issues, it doesn't need much more than simple basic web app security.  Votes can be stored non-anonymously in the database at first, and people can use an ID like a driver's license to register until funding is acquired.

So if we can get a platform built, then the next question is how do we get people registered on the platform, and how do we get representatives?

First off, I think people will need to subscribe to their local channel.  Hopefully we can get volunteers, or preferably raise money to pay people to enter their local & regional votes into the system.   As a potential stream of revenue, we could also charge neighborhood associations, or any group who needs a voting system a subscription to use the platform, and users can register and subscribe to their local & regional chapters after verifying that they are registered to vote there.  Perhaps they should be able to vote, but it just won't count for the representative until they are registered as open party.  As far as representatives, I think we can probably get some people already elected to local offices to join.  Otherwise we just put some funding or volunteer work toward local elections where people are active on the platform & hope that it can gain momentum after that.
