In this [review](http://ase.tufts.edu/cogstud/dennett/papers/achingvoids.pdf) of Incomplete Nature by Daniel Dennett, he makes a pretty apt comparison between the "romantic" and "enlightenment" thinkers.  This seems to reflect an ideological divide I previously had no name for.

I'm not sure I can quite put my finger on what distinguishes the two, but I can definitely categorize myself on the romantic side and Dawkins would be far on the enlightenment side.  Because of this, I've often talked smack about Dawkins, but the truth is, I've never read him.  So a friend who is a fan of his got me "the selfish gene" and I'm reading it for the sake of exposure to a dissenting worldview.
